Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Help: The Free Remediation Program

April 29, 2026 by LongPro Pest Control

Cuyahoga County offers free bed bug treatment to eligible seniors and adults with disabilities through its Bed Bug Remediation Program. Eligibility, contact numbers, and how to apply.

Bed bug treatment can run hundreds to thousands of dollars, and that's a real problem for residents on fixed incomes. The good news for Cuyahoga County is that there's a county-run program that pays for treatment for eligible residents — and many people who qualify don't realize it exists.

This guide explains the Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Remediation Program in plain language: who qualifies, what it covers, how to apply, and which phone numbers to call. We're not the program ourselves — Cuyahoga County Health and Human Services administers it — but we believe nobody should be living with bed bugs because they can't afford a quote, so we want eligible residents to know this exists.

What the Program Offers

The Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Remediation Program provides up to three free visits from a licensed extermination company to eligible residents. The county pays the treatment company directly — there's no out-of-pocket cost to the resident.

Three visits is the standard professional protocol for residential bed bug elimination: an initial treatment, a follow-up roughly two weeks later to break the egg cycle, and a final inspection to confirm no live activity remains. The program covers all three.

According to the county, more than 100 households have been treated through the program since it launched. It is administered jointly by Cuyahoga County Health and Human Services (Division of Senior & Adult Services) and the Cuyahoga County Board of Health.

Who Qualifies

The program is targeted at residents who would otherwise struggle to afford treatment. Eligibility is based on three things:

Age or disability status. You must be either age 60 or older, OR an adult age 18 to 59 receiving disability payments. The program is specifically designed for older adults and adults with disabilities.

Residency. You must live in Cuyahoga County, and you must live in the home that needs treatment. Renters and homeowners both qualify. The program covers single and double housing units (single-family homes and two-family duplexes).

Income. Your household income must fall under the program's income guidelines. Based on the program's most recent published information, the limits range roughly from about $35,000 for a single-person household to about $71,500 for a four-person household. Income limits update periodically — the application paperwork will have the current numbers, and program staff can confirm whether you qualify when you call.

If you're outside the eligibility criteria but still need help affording treatment, talk to the program when you call — they may be able to refer you to other community resources.

How to Apply

The application starts with a phone call. There are two numbers, both belonging to the same county program:

  • Cuyahoga County Health and Human Services — Senior & Adult Services: (216) 420-6750. Address: 13815 Kinsman Rd., Cleveland, OH 44120.
  • Cuyahoga County Board of Health: (216) 201-2000 ext. 1264.

Either number is fine to start with — they'll route you to the right intake person.

What to have ready when you call:

  • Photo ID for the applicant.
  • Proof of residency (recent utility bill, lease, or property tax bill in the applicant's name at the address being treated).
  • Income verification for everyone in the household (recent pay stubs, Social Security or disability award letters, pension statements — whatever applies).
  • If applying based on disability rather than age, documentation of the disability payment (SSDI, SSI, VA disability, etc.).

The intake person will tell you whether to mail or drop off the paperwork, schedule a confirmation visit, or fill out a form online. Don't worry about getting all the documents perfect before you call — the call is to find out what they actually need from your situation.

What to Expect During Treatment

Once your application is approved, the county dispatches an approved licensed extermination company to your home. The treatment process is the same as any professional residential bed bug job — a thorough inspection, two treatment visits 10 to 14 days apart to break the egg cycle, and a final inspection visit to confirm elimination.

The county pays the extermination company directly. You don't write any checks. You don't pay any deposit. The only thing the program asks of you is that you complete the prep the technician asks for (typically washing and heat-drying clothing and bedding, decluttering harborage areas, and being available during the scheduled visits).

For more on what the prep work looks like, see our guide on how to prepare for bed bug treatment and what to expect during the inspection. The same protocols apply whether you're going through the county program or hiring privately.

If You Don't Qualify for the Program

If you're under 60, not on disability, or above the income limits, the county program isn't for you — but you have other options:

About the Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Task Force

The county program is connected to the Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Task Force, a coordinating body of local health departments, pest control operators, and community organizations that has been meeting since 2011 to address bed bugs as a public health issue. The task force hosts informational events and maintains educational materials at cuyahogabedbugs.org.

If you're a Cuyahoga County resident dealing with bed bugs and you want to learn more before applying or hiring anyone, the task force website has practical guidance under "Dos & Don'ts." The CDC and U.S. EPA classify bed bugs as a pest of significant public health importance — meaning the county takes this seriously and so should you.

If you or someone you know in Cuyahoga County qualifies for the Bed Bug Remediation Program, please share this page or call the county directly at (216) 420-6750. The program exists to help, and the bigger problem is that not enough eligible residents know about it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Cuyahoga County bed bug program actually free? +

Yes, fully free for residents who qualify. The county pays the licensed extermination company directly. There's no out-of-pocket cost, no deposit, no later bill. The only commitment is completing the prep work the technician asks for and being available for the scheduled treatment visits.

Who is eligible for the Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Remediation Program? +

Cuyahoga County residents who are either 60 or older, OR adults 18-59 receiving disability payments, with household income under the program's published guidelines (approximately $35,000 to $71,500 depending on household size as of the most recently published numbers). You must live in the home being treated, in single or double housing.

How do I apply for free bed bug treatment in Cuyahoga County? +

Call Cuyahoga County HHS Senior & Adult Services at (216) 420-6750, or the Cuyahoga County Board of Health at (216) 201-2000 ext. 1264. Either line connects you to the right program staff. Have photo ID, proof of residency, household income documentation, and (if applicable) proof of disability ready when you call.

Does the program cover apartment buildings? +

The program covers single and double housing units — single-family homes and two-family duplexes. Larger apartment buildings have different treatment dynamics that fall outside the program's scope. If you're in a larger building, ask the program for referrals to other resources, and review our apartment-specific guide for your rights as an Ohio renter.

Can LongPro Pest Control treat me through the county program? +

Not yet — we are not currently an approved vendor for the county program, though it's something we're looking into. If you qualify for the program, the county will assign you to one of its approved licensed extermination companies. If you'd rather hire privately, LongPro offers free inspections and written estimates with no pressure.
