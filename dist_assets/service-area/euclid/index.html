Why Euclid Has Unique Pest Challenges

Euclid is one of Cleveland's largest inner-ring suburbs, with a diverse housing stock that includes single-family homes, duplexes, and apartment complexes. Many homes were built in the 1940s–1960s, and the city's density — especially along major corridors like Euclid Avenue, Lake Shore Boulevard, and East 222nd Street — creates conditions where pests can spread between properties.

Bed bugs are a particular concern in Euclid's multi-family housing, where infestations can travel between units through shared walls and plumbing. Cockroaches and ants are also common in Euclid's older homes, where gaps in foundations and aging weatherstripping provide easy entry points.
