Slavic Village and Broadway: Cleveland's Historic Southeast Side

Slavic Village is one of Cleveland's oldest and most historically rich neighborhoods. The area grew around Czech and Polish immigrant communities and still carries that older housing stock today. Many of the homes, duplexes, and mixed-use properties in the neighborhood have the character people love, but they also have more seams, gaps, and aging utility penetrations than newer construction.

Broadway and Fleet Avenue create a dense residential-commercial corridor where pest problems can move faster than owners expect. Apartment buildings, side-by-side duplexes, and closely spaced houses make shared-wall and neighboring-unit issues more common, especially with bed bugs and cockroaches.

LongPro is based in Old Brooklyn, just across the valley. That nearby location matters here. We can usually respond quickly, we know the housing stock, and we understand how to treat these properties without turning the service call into a public spectacle.

Why Pest Problems Show Up Faster Here

Older construction gives pests more entry points

Aging foundations, settled walls, older basements, and decades of repairs create more access points for ants, spiders, cockroaches, and mice activity than newer homes usually have. We inspect for the actual entry conditions instead of just spraying and leaving.

Shared walls make apartment issues harder to isolate

In duplexes and multi-family buildings, pests do not respect unit boundaries. Bed bugs, cockroaches, and occasional invaders can move between walls, utility chases, and common areas. That is why Slavic Village problems often need a more coordinated treatment plan.

Historic homes need targeted treatment, not overkill

We do not treat older homes like blank boxes. Slavic Village properties often have original trim, older flooring transitions, and irregular void spaces. We build the treatment around the structure so you get real control without unnecessary chemical use.

Property managers need something workable

If you own or manage a multi-unit building in the area, the goal is not a one-off visit that looks good on paper. The goal is a building that stays under control. We can handle inspections, unit coordination, and recurring follow-up when the problem calls for it.
