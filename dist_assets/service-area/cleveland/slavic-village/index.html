Slavic Village & Broadway: Cleveland's Historic Southeast Side

Neighborhood History

Slavic Village and Broadway represent one of Cleveland's oldest and most historically rich neighborhoods. Originally settled in 1799 as the township of Newburgh, the area became the heart of Czech and Polish immigrant communities in the late 1800s and early 1900s.

Centered around E. 65th and 71st Streets between Broadway and Harvard Avenues, along Fleet Avenue, this neighborhood is characterized by multi-family homes, duplexes, and historic single-family residences that tell the story of Cleveland's industrial heritage.

Modern Revitalization

In recent years, Slavic Village has been undergoing exciting revitalization. New investment, community gardens, and bicycle trails are bringing fresh energy to the neighborhood while honoring its rich heritage.

The area's proximity to downtown Cleveland, the Cuyahoga Valley National Park, and the Towpath Trail makes it increasingly attractive to residents and visitors. Close to I-77 and I-490, Slavic Village offers both neighborhood character and convenient access to regional destinations.

Older Housing = More Pest Problems

Slavic Village's historic charm comes with a common challenge: aging housing stock naturally attracts and harbors pests.

Why Older Homes Attract Pests

  • ✓ Cracks and gaps in aging foundations
  • ✓ Deteriorated exterior walls and siding
  • ✓ Compromised pipes and utility entry points
  • ✓ Warped doors and window frames
  • ✓ Hidden voids in walls and crawl spaces
  • ✓ Decades of accumulated hiding places

Multi-Family Building Challenges

  • ✓ Infestations spread quickly between units
  • ✓ Shared walls and ceilings
  • ✓ Common area pest problems
  • ✓ Coordination challenges with tenants
  • ✓ Pipes and vents connecting multiple units
  • ✓ Need for building-wide treatment plans
