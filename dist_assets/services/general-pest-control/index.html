Common Cleveland Household Pests

Cleveland's climate and architecture create ideal conditions for a variety of household pests. Here's what we commonly treat:

Ants

Carpenter ants, pavement ants, and odorous house ants are a persistent problem in Cleveland. We identify the species, locate their entry points, and eliminate them at the source.

Spiders

While most spiders are harmless and even beneficial, their webs and occasional bites create a pest control need. We remove them humanely and safely.

Silverfish & Earwigs

These moisture-loving pests thrive in basements and bathrooms. They damage paper goods and fabrics. We target their habitats and eliminate the problem.

Centipedes

Fast-moving and unsettling, centipedes hunt other insects. We eliminate them and reduce the pests they feed on, creating an unfavorable environment.

Stink Bugs

These seasonal invaders appear in fall and emerge in spring. We provide targeted treatments during peak invasion periods to keep them out.

Occasional Invaders

Millipedes, pill bugs, and other occasional invaders seek shelter during weather changes. We seal entry points and treat perimeters as needed.

When Do You Actually Need Pest Control?

Cleveland's seasons create distinct pest patterns. Understanding when pests are most active helps you know when to call:

Spring (March–May)

Carpenter ants and pavement ants become active as temperatures warm. This is the most common time for ant invasions. Early treatment prevents larger problems.

Summer (June–August)

Most pest activity is at baseline. Unless you have an active infestation, summer is usually quiet. Maintenance or prevention treatments are optional.

Fall (September–November)

Stink bugs, ladybugs, and other occasional invaders seek warmth. Fall is the second-busiest season for pest control. Perimeter treatment before October helps prevent invasions.

Winter (December–February)

Most outdoor pests go dormant. Indoor pest activity drops significantly unless you have an established problem (rodents, cockroaches). Maintenance is generally unnecessary.
