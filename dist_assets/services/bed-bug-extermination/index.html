Signs You Have Bed Bugs

Bed bug infestations aren't always obvious at first, but there are several telltale signs that should prompt immediate action. Recognizing these signs early allows you to address the problem before the infestation spreads to other areas of your home.

Bite Patterns

Itchy bites in clusters, lines, or groups on exposed skin, especially on arms, legs, and neck. Bites typically appear within hours to days of exposure.

Bloodstains on Sheets

Small reddish-brown spots or smears on pillowcases, mattresses, and bedding where bed bugs have been crushed or fed.

Visible Eggs and Shells

Tiny white or cream-colored eggs and translucent shed exoskeletons found in mattress seams, box springs, and furniture crevices.

Musty Odor

A sweet, musty scent in heavily infested areas. This odor comes from bed bug pheromones and is often one of the first indicators.

Live Bugs in Crevices

Small, flat, reddish-brown insects about the size of an apple seed found in mattress seams, box springs, headboards, and wall cracks.

How to Prepare for Treatment

Preparation is key to treatment success. When you schedule your service, we provide a detailed preparation checklist. Here are the essential steps to ready your home:

  • • Strip all bedding and wash everything in hot water (at least 130°F). Dry on high heat for at least 30 minutes.
  • • Clear clutter from around beds and bedroom furniture to ensure we can access all areas where bed bugs hide.
  • • Vacuum thoroughly all carpeted areas, especially around beds and furniture. Dispose of the vacuum bag immediately in a sealed plastic bag outside.
  • • Move furniture at least 2 feet away from walls to allow access for inspection and treatment.
  • • Remove pets from the home during treatment and for the specified re-entry period — typically 2-4 hours.
  • • Ensure access to all rooms — we need to inspect and potentially treat every area of your home.
