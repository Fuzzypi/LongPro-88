VA & WDI Home Loan Inspections in Cleveland, OH

Buying, selling, or refinancing a home in Greater Cleveland often requires a Wood-Destroying Insect (WDI) inspection before the loan can close. LongPro Pest Control provides certified WDI inspections and the official report your lender needs — scheduled around your closing date and delivered fast. This is an inspection and reporting service, not termite treatment.

Important: We identify and document wood-destroying insect activity for your loan or closing. We do not perform termite extermination — if active WDI activity is found, your report spells out exactly what was identified so you and your lender can decide next steps.

What Is a WDI Inspection?

A Wood-Destroying Insect inspection is a professional evaluation of a property for evidence of insects that damage wood — termites, carpenter ants, carpenter bees, and powderpost beetles — plus related conditions like wood-decay fungus and moisture issues that attract them. The inspector documents findings on the industry-standard NPMA-33 report, the form most lenders and the VA require.

It is most commonly ordered as part of a real estate transaction. The report confirms, in writing, whether visible evidence of wood-destroying insects was found at the time of inspection.

When You Need One

VA & FHA Home Loans

Government-backed loans typically require a clear WDI report before closing.

Home Purchase

Buyers and lenders want written confirmation the home is free of active WDI damage.

Refinancing

Some refinance and assumption packages call for a current WDI report.

Seller Disclosure

Sellers order ahead of listing to avoid closing-day surprises.

What We Inspect For

  • •Termites — subterranean and other species, including mud tubes, damaged wood, and swarmer evidence.
  • •Carpenter ants — galleries and frass that signal structural wood activity.
  • •Carpenter bees — boring holes in eaves, decks, and trim.
  • •Powderpost beetles — exit holes and powder in hardwood and framing.
  • •Wood-decay fungus & moisture — conditions conducive to wood-destroying organisms.

The Official NPMA-33 Report

After the inspection you receive the completed NPMA-33 form — the standardized document lenders, the VA, title companies, and real estate agents recognize. It records what was inspected, what (if anything) was found, and any conditions conducive to infestation.

We send the report quickly so it never becomes the reason your closing slips.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a WDI inspection and why does my lender want one?

A Wood-Destroying Insect inspection checks a property for termites and other wood-damaging insects and reports the findings on the NPMA-33 form. Lenders — especially for VA and FHA loans — require it to confirm the home has no active, undisclosed insect damage before they finance it.

Do I need a WDI inspection for a VA loan in Ohio?

In most cases yes. VA loans generally require a clear WDI report in Ohio. Your lender or agent will confirm, and we provide the exact report they're looking for.

If you find termites, do you treat them?

No — we're an inspection and reporting service, not a termite-treatment company. We document exactly what we find so you and your lender know the situation, and you can arrange treatment with a qualified termite specialist if needed.

How fast can I get the report?

We schedule around your closing timeline and deliver the completed NPMA-33 report quickly — usually within a day or two of the inspection. Call us with your deadline and we'll work to meet it.
