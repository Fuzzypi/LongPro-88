**Why:** Aligns LongPro with a real public-good program. Low-income / disabled / 60+ residents in Cuyahoga County who can't afford bed bug treatment can get it free through the county. Becoming an approved vendor lets LongPro do that work, and gives the marketing site a stronger CTA on the Cuyahoga-county-help blog post.

## Numbers to call

- **Cuyahoga County Board of Health (CCBH)** — primary line: **(216) 201-2000 ext. 1264** (or ext. 1200)
- **Cuyahoga County HHS, Senior & Adult Services** — **(216) 420-6750** (13815 Kinsman Rd., Cleveland 44120)

Either is a reasonable starting point. CCBH ext. 1264 is the line that has historically run the Bed Bug Task Force; HHS Senior & Adult Services administers the actual remediation benefits.

## What to ask

> "I'm calling about the Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Remediation Program. We're a licensed, BBB A+ pest control company based in Cleveland — LongPro Pest Control. How do I become an approved vendor for the program, and what paperwork or qualifications do you need from us?"

If they don't run vendor enrollment themselves, they'll refer you to the right office.

## Expected info they'll need

- Ohio commercial pest applicator license number (LongPro is licensed)
- Insurance (general liability + workers comp) — provide certificate
- W-9 / vendor tax info
- Possibly a service-area declaration (we serve all of Cuyahoga County)
- Possibly a price sheet for residential bed bug treatments

## Optional — Cuyahoga County Bed Bug Task Force

The task force at https://www.cuyahogabedbugs.org/ is a coordinating body of local health departments and pest control operators. Their member list is at /list-of-members. The site looks dormant (last conference 2019, last announced meeting Jan 2024) but membership is still a useful network connection. Ask CCBH about the task force when you call.

## Program facts (verified 2026-04-29 via web search)

- Adults 60+ OR adults 18–59 with documented disability
- Must reside in Cuyahoga County, single/double housing units
- Income limits approx. $35,010 (single) → $71,550 (4-person)
- Up to 3 free visits by licensed extermination company
- 100+ households served since program inception
- Application form revised July 2023, hosted at cleveland.gov
